GRASP Metaheuristics - Postgraduate Project

Combinatorial optimization is a vast field of applied mathematics, which from a computational perspective seeks to develop algorithmic solutions to problems where it is required to optimize (maximize or minimize) an objective function.
Metaheuristics are heuristic methods (based on experience and its consequences) to solve a type of general computational problem, using the parameters given by the user on generic and abstract procedures in a way that is expected to be efficient.
The GRASP method can solve a wide variety of problems efficiently. The use of Python in this field, with data structures and specific commands, such as those we obtain through NumPy, Matplotlib, Random, and other modules, are of special importance for solving real-world problems, which seek low-cost solutions
